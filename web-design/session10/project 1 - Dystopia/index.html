Cyberpunk

Cyberpunk is a dystopian vision of the future where advanced technology, often centered around cybernetics and artificial intelligence, coexists with societal decay and corporate domination. Set in gritty urban landscapes, cyberpunk worlds are characterized by neon-lit streets, towering skyscrapers, and a stark divide between the wealthy elite and the impoverished masses. Technology is both a tool of empowerment and oppression, with characters often hacking into systems or augmenting their bodies with cybernetics to survive. The themes of cyberpunk revolve around the loss of individuality, the blurring line between human and machine, and the consequences of unchecked technological advancement.

Movie: Blade Runner (1982)
Anime: Akira (1988)
TV Series: Altered Carbon (2018-2020)
Novel: Snow Crash (1992)
Video Game: Cyberpunk 2077 (2020)

Atompunk

Atompunk is a retro-futuristic genre rooted in the mid-20th century, reflecting the optimism and anxieties of the Atomic Age. It draws inspiration from the post-World War II era, particularly the 1950s and 1960s, when the world was fascinated by nuclear power, space exploration, and the potential of modern science. Atompunk worlds are characterized by sleek, futuristic designs, ray guns, space rockets, and the iconic imagery of atomic energy. The genre explores the tension between the promise of scientific progress and the fear of nuclear annihilation, often incorporating elements of Cold War paranoia and mid-century modern aesthetics. Atompunk is a nostalgic reflection on a time when the future seemed both hright.

Movie: Love the Bomb (1964)
Movie: Tomorrowland (2015)
Movie: The Iron Giant (1999)
Short Story Collection: Go Down Together (1976)
Video Game: Bioshock (2007)

Solarpunk

Solarpunk is a utopian vision of the future that emphasizes sustainability, renewable energy, and harmonious coexistence with nature. Unlike the darker tones of cyberpunk, solarpunk is optimistic, imagining a world where humanity has successfully overcome the environmental challenges of the past. Cities in solarpunk worlds are often lush with greenery, powered by solar, wind, and other renewable sources. The architecture blends modern technology with organic forms, creating spaces that are both functional and beautiful. Solarpunk explores themes of community, environmental stewardship, and the balance between technology and nature, offering a hopeful glimpse of a future where progress and ecology are intertwined.

Documentary: 2040 (2019)
Novel: The Ministry for the Future (2020)
Novel: Ecotopia (1975)
Novel: The Fifth Sacred Thing (1993)
Anthology: A Sustainable World (2018)

Steampunk

Steampunk is a retro-futuristic genre that imagines a world where steam power remains the dominant technology, often set in an alternate 19th century or a Victorian-inspired future. This world is filled with intricate clockwork mechanisms, hrass gadgets, and airships floating in the sky. Steampunk blends historical elements with speculative technology, creating a unique aesthetic where the elegance of the past meets the wonder of invention. It explores themes of industrial revolution, class struggle, and the romance of exploration, all while maintaining a sense of adventure and discovery. The characters in steampunk worlds are often inventors, explorers, or revolutionaries, navigating a society on the cusp of change.

Movie: The Golden Compass (2007)
Movie: Mortal Engines (2018)
Video Game: Bioshock Infinite (2013)
Novel: Leviathan (2009)
Manga/Anime: Fullmetal Alchemist (2001-2010)
